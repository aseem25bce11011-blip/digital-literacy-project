Cybercrime in India has been increasing rapidly, especially in the form of UPI and online payment fraud, where criminals use fear and manipulation to extract money from victims. One emerging method is the “digital arrest” scam, in which fraudsters impersonate law enforcement officials and falsely accuse individuals of serious crimes such as money laundering. The primary goal of such scams is to pressure victims into transferring money under false pretenses.

In these cases, the fraudsters contact the victim through phone calls or video calls and create a sense of urgency and fear. They may claim that the victim is under investigation and must cooperate immediately. Victims are often isolated and instructed not to contact anyone else. Under pressure, they are forced to transfer money, believing it is required for verification or to avoid legal consequences.

Such scams commonly target individuals who are unaware of digital fraud tactics and legal procedures. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and loss of trust in digital platforms.

A real-life example of this scam occurred in Gurugram, where a man was cheated of ₹2 lakh. Cybercriminals posed as police officers and falsely accused him of money laundering. They placed him under “digital arrest” through video calls and threatened legal consequences. Under pressure, the victim transferred the money to the fraudsters.
